# Growth principles

- Growth is repeated delivery and expansion of customer value, not only acquisition.
- A metric is useful when its definition, owner, data source, window, and decision are clear.
- The highest-leverage constraint may be measurement, activation, operations, retention, or monetization—not the loudest channel.
- Experiments reduce uncertainty. They do not manufacture certainty.
- A winning result is not a universal truth; record the conditions under which it held.
- Capacity is a constraint. Work in progress should be limited.
- Durable growth protects user autonomy, trust, accessibility, privacy, and brand.
